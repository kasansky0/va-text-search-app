ISSUES 

Entitlement to service connection for sleep apnea.

Entitlement to service connection for lumbosacral strain, claimed as mechanical low back pain.

ORDERS

Entitlement to service connection for sleep apnea is WITHDRAWN.

Entitlement to service connection for lumbosacral strain is WITHDRAWN.

FINDING OF FACT

On January 28, 2025, prior to the issuance of a decision in the appeal, the Veteran withdrew the appeal of service connection for sleep apnea and lumbosacral strain.

CONCLUSIONS OF LAW

The criteria for withdrawal of the appeal for service connection for sleep apnea are met. 38 C.F.R. § 20.205.

The criteria for withdrawal of the appeal for service connection for lumbosacral strain are met. 38 C.F.R. § 20.205.

FACTUAL AND PROCEDURAL HISTORY

The Veteran served on active duty in the Marine Corps from November 1992 to November 1996 and the United States Army from February 1997 to November 1999. The matter comes to the Board of Veterans' Appeals (Board) on appeal from two June 2023 rating decisions issued by a Regional Office (RO) of the Department of Veterans Affairs (VA).

The appeal was prosecuted pursuant to the modernized review system.

REASONS AND BASES FOR THE FINDING AND CONCLUSIONS

Legal Criteria

An appeal may be withdrawn as to any or all issues involved in the appeal at any time before the Board issues a decision. 38 C.F.R. § 20.205(a), (b)(3). Withdrawal may be made by the appellant or by his or her authorized representative. Id. § 20.205(a). Except for appeals withdrawn on the record at a hearing, appeal withdrawals must be filed with the Board in writing and are effective upon receipt. Id. § 20.205(b)(2), (b)(3).

A written withdrawal must include (1) the name of the veteran or the name of the claimant or appellant if other than the veteran, (2) the applicable VA file number, and (3) a statement that the appeal is withdrawn. If the appeal involves multiple issues, the withdrawal must specify that the appeal is withdrawn in its entirety or list the issue(s) withdrawn from the appeal. Id. § 20.205(b)(1).

Analysis

Here, the Veteran submitted a written request to withdraw this appeal on January 28, 2025. Specifically, the Veteran stated "I herein withdraw all pending appeals as I am now 100% P&T, effective February 11, 2024. Thank you."

The Veteran's request included his name, his VA file number, and a statement that the appeal is withdrawn.

The Veteran's statements are clear that he wishes to withdraw his appeal in whole. The withdrawal request was received prior to the issuance of a decision on the merits of the claim by the Board.

The Board concludes the requirements to perfect a withdrawal as to the issue of service connection for sleep apnea and lumbosacral strain are met, and the instant appeal is withdrawn. 38 C.F.R. § 20.205.
