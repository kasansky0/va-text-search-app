ORDER

Eligibility to attorney fees, based on past-due benefits awarded from a June 2024 rating decision, is dismissed.

?

FINDING OF FACT

In October 2024, prior to the promulgation of a decision in the appeal, the appellant withdrew the issue of attorney fees.

CONCLUSION OF LAW

The criteria for withdrawal of an appeal by the appellant have been met.  38 U.S.C. § 7105; 38 C.F.R. § 20.205.

REASONS AND BASES FOR FINDING AND CONCLUSION

The Veteran served on active duty from October 2010 to June 2011.  

The appellant is the Veteran's former attorney representative.

This matter is before the Board on appeal from a June 2024 fee decision.

In an August 2024 notice of disagreement (NOD), the Veteran's representative appealed and elected the Direct Review docket.

Attorney fees based on past-due benefits awarded from a June 2024 rating decision.

The Board may dismiss any appeal which fails to allege specific error of fact or law in the determination being appealed.  38 U.S.C. § 7105.  An appeal may be withdrawn as to any or all issues involved in the appeal at any time before the Board promulgates a decision.  38 C.F.R. § 20.205.  Withdrawal may be made by the appellant or by his or her authorized representative.  Id. 

In the present contested claim case, the appellant is the Veteran's former attorney representative, and withdrew the appeal for attorney fees in writing in October 2024.  There are no allegations of errors of fact or law for appellate consideration.  Accordingly, the appeal is dismissed.
