ORDER

The appeal regarding the propriety of the removal of the Veteran's dependent child, N., from his award effective January 1, 2023, is dismissed as a matter of law. 

FINDING OF FACT

1. In the January 2023 decision on appeal, VA notified the Veteran that his dependent child, N., was removed from his award effective January 1, 2023, because he failed to verify the status of his dependent child.

2. In a June 14, 2023, letter, VA notified that his dependent child, N., who had been removed from his award, was added back to his award effective the original effective date of May 31, 2014. 

CONCLUSION OF LAW

As the benefits sought on appeal have been granted, there remains no case or controversy as to the appeal regarding the propriety of the removal of the Veteran's dependent child, N., from his award effective January 1, 2023. 38 U.S.C. ยง 7105. 

REASONS AND BASES FOR FINDING AND CONCLUSION

The Veteran served on active duty from August 2001 to May 2014. 

This matter comes before the Board of Veterans' Appeals (Board) on appeal from Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) Regional Office (RO)'s decision issued in January 2023. In the February 2023 VA Form 10182, Decision Review Request: Board Appeal (Notice of Disagreement), the Veteran elected the Direct Review docket.

In the January 2023 decision on appeal, VA notified the Veteran that his dependent child, N., was removed from his award effective January 1, 2023, because he failed to verify the status of the dependent child. 

In February 2023, the Veteran contacted VA and verified the current status of his dependent child N.  

In a June 14, 2023, notification letter, VA notified that his dependent child, N., who had been removed from his award effective January 1, 2023, were added back to her award effective May 31, 2014, the original effective date of the addition of N. to his award.
