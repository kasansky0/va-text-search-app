ORDER

The appeal seeking additional attorney fees based on past-due benefits awarded in a September 2024 rating decision is dismissed.

FINDING OF FACT

Prior to the promulgation of a decision in the appeal, the Board of Veterans' Appeals (Board) received notice from the appellant that he wished to withdraw his appeal.

CONCLUSION OF LAW

The criteria for withdrawal of the appeal seeking additional attorney fees based on past-due benefits awarded in a September 2024 rating decision have been met.  38 U.S.C. § 7105; 38 C.F.R. § 20.205.

REASONS AND BASES FOR FINDING AND CONCLUSION

The Board may dismiss any appeal which fails to allege specific error of fact or law in the determination being appealed.?  38?U.S.C. §?7105.?  An appeal may be withdrawn as to any or all issues involved in the appeal at any time before the Board promulgates a decision.?38?C.F.R. §?20.205.? Withdrawal may be made by the appellant or by their authorized representative.  Id.  An appeal withdrawal is effective when received by the Board.?  38?C.F.R. §?20.205(b)(3).  The withdrawal must include the name of the appellant, the applicable file number, and a statement that the appeal is being withdrawn.  38 C.F.R. § 20.205; see Hembree v. Wilkie, 33 Vet. App. 1, 6 (2020).

In an October 2024 Fee Decision, the appellant was notified that eligibility for attorney fees based on past-due benefits awarded in a September 2024 rating decision had been granted; the specified fees totaled $2,172.07.  In a December 2024 VA Form 10182, the appellant sought Board review of the Fee Decision, contending that the amount of attorney fees had been improperly calculated.

However, in a December 2024 correspondence, the appellant notified the Board that he wished to withdraw his December 2024 appeal as to the calculation of attorney fees.  See December 2024 Correspondence.

Given that the withdrawal request includes the name of the appellant, the applicable file number, and a statement that the appeal is being withdrawn, the Board finds that the appeal seeking additional attorney fees based on past-due benefits awarded in a September 2024 rating decision to be withdrawn.  See Hembree v. Wilkie, supra.
