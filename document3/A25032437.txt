ORDER

The appeal is dismissed.

FINDING OF FACT

In an April 2025 statement, prior to the promulgation of a decision in the appeal, the Veteran expressed the desire to withdraw the appeal of the rating assigned for allergic rhinitis.

CONCLUSION OF LAW

The criteria for withdrawal of the appeal have been met. 38 U.S.C. ยง 7105 (2018); 38 C.F.R. ยง 20.205 (2024).

REASONS AND BASES FOR FINDING AND CONCLUSION

The Board of Veterans' Appeals (Board) may dismiss any appeal which fails to allege specific error of fact or law in the determination being appealed. 38 U.S.C. 7105 (d). An appeal may be withdrawn as to any or all issues involved in the appeal at any time before the Board promulgates a decision. 38 C.F.R. 20.205. Withdrawal may be made by the appellant or by his or her authorized representative. 38 C.F.R. 20.205.

In an April 2025 statements, the Veteran withdrew the appeal of the denial of entitlement to an increased rating for allergic rhinitis. In the written withdrawal, the Veteran specifically reference the appeal related to the VA Form 10182 received by the Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) on February 6, 2024. As such, there remain no allegations of errors of fact or law for appellate consideration. Accordingly, the Board does not have jurisdiction to review the issue, and it is dismissed.
