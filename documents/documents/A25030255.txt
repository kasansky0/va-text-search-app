ORDER

The appeal regarding the propriety of the removal of the Veteran's dependents from her award effective November 1, 2023, is dismissed as a matter of law. 

FINDINGS OF FACT

1. In the October 2023 decision on appeal, VA notified the Veteran that her three dependents were removed from her award effective November 1, 2023, because she failed to submit a dependency verification form.

2. In an April 15, 2024, notification letter, VA notified that her dependents who had been removed from her award effective November 1, 2023, were added back to her award effective November 1, 2023. 

CONCLUSION OF LAW

As the benefits sought on appeal have been granted, there remains no case or controversy as to the appeal regarding the propriety of the removal of the Veteran's dependents from her award effective November 1, 2023. 38 U.S.C. ยง 7105.

REASONS AND BASES FOR FINDINGS AND CONCLUSION

The Veteran served on active duty from May 1998 to May 2006. 

This matter comes before the Board of Veterans' Appeals (Board) on appeal from Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) Regional Office (RO)'s decision issued in October 2023; the modernized review system, also known as the Appeals Modernization Act (AMA), applies. In the November 2023 VA Form 10182, Decision Review Request: Board Appeal (Notice of Disagreement), the Veteran elected the Direct Review docket.

In the October 2023 decision on appeal, VA notified the Veteran that her three dependents were removed from her award effective November 1, 2023, because she failed to submit a dependency verification form. 

In November 2023, the Veteran submitted a completed VA Form 21-686c. 

In an April 15, 2024, notification letter, VA notified that her dependents who had been removed from her award effective November 1, 2023, were added back to her award effective November 1, 2023.
